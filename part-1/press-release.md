## GOES Announces Multi-Year Cloud Transformation Programme to Accelerate Global Growth and Operational Excellence

*Company appoints industry veteran Marcus Webb as Chief Technology Officer to lead strategic initiative*

**NEW YORK, 14 January 2026** — GOES (Global Online E-Commerce and Streaming), the multinational commerce and content platform serving 45 million monthly active users across 28 countries, today announced a strategic multi-year programme to migrate its technology infrastructure to a hybrid cloud architecture.

The programme, backed by the company's board of directors, is designed to accelerate GOES's ability to bring new products and services to market, improve the resilience and scalability of its platform, and deliver a superior experience for its growing customer base.

"Our customers expect the best — seamless shopping, uninterrupted streaming, and instant access to the live events they love," said Jonathan Pierce, CEO of GOES. "This investment in our technology foundation is a commitment to delivering on that expectation at a scale and speed that our current infrastructure simply cannot support. It is the right decision for our customers, our partners, and our shareholders."

GOES simultaneously announced the appointment of Marcus Webb as Chief Technology Officer, effective January 2026. Mr. Webb brings over 20 years of experience leading large-scale infrastructure and platform engineering organisations, including senior roles at two of the world's largest technology companies.

"I joined GOES because I believe this platform has the potential to be the infrastructure behind the future of commerce and live entertainment," said Mr. Webb. "Cloud-native architecture is not a destination — it is a capability we need to build into everything we do. The talent, the ambition, and now the mandate are in place. I'm excited to get to work."

The transformation programme will be delivered in partnership with leading cloud and technology providers. Further details on partnership arrangements will be announced in due course.

**About the Initiative**

The programme encompasses GOES's e-commerce, streaming, and live-event ticketing platforms, as well as the company's proprietary payments infrastructure. The company stated that business continuity will be maintained throughout the migration, with no planned service interruptions. A phased delivery model will be used to manage risk.

GOES serves customers in North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific and operates infrastructure across multiple continents to meet regional data residency requirements. The company processes hundreds of millions of customer transactions annually.

**Financial Guidance**

GOES confirmed that the transformation programme is fully funded and is expected to deliver material reductions in infrastructure operating costs over a multi-year horizon. The company will provide updated guidance at its Q1 2026 earnings call.

**About GOES**

GOES is a global technology company operating one of the world's largest combined commerce and content platforms. Founded in 2004 and publicly traded on the NYSE, GOES connects consumers with products, entertainment, and live experiences in 28 markets worldwide.
